**McKinsey-grade strategic thinking, codified into ten skills you can run on demand.**

Most strategy work fails in the same places: a misdiagnosed starting point, a metric system that produces motion but not progress, a recommendation that gets killed in the room because no one aligned the swing votes first. These ten skills compress the methodology a senior management consultant would bring to those problems — Pyramid Principle, MECE issue trees, Porter's Five Forces, Theory of Constraints, pre-mortem logic, war-gaming — into structured workflows Claude can execute alongside you.

The skills split into two halves. **Diagnosis** sharpens the thinking before you commit. **Execution** carries the strategy across the finish line. Each skill includes the workflow a senior associate would walk through, the hypotheses to pressure-test, the quality checks before sharing work upward, and the common failure modes to avoid.

**How to use them:** drop the folder into Claude, point it at the question you are working on, and ask. No retainer. No two-hundred-slide deck. No six-month engagement. Just rigor, on demand.

## Skills

### Diagnosis — sharpen the thinking before you commit

| Skill | What it does |
|---|---|
| Situation Assessment | Establish a defensible, fact-based truth about where the business actually is — across financials, market position, and momentum |
| Competitive Intel | Model what rivals will do next based on capabilities and incentives, and pre-commit responses before they move |
| Market Mapping | Size the market bottom-up and top-down, segment it MECE, and identify the white space competitors have missed |
| Assumption Audit | Surface the load-bearing beliefs underneath a strategy, grade the evidence, and force weak assumptions into testable form |
| Growth Barriers | Identify the real constraint holding growth back — not the symptom leadership is talking about |

### Execution — carry the strategy across the finish line

| Skill | What it does |
|---|---|
| Initiative Prioritizer | Cut a list of twenty initiatives down to the three that matter, sequence them, and produce a defensible kill list |
| Stakeholder Alignment | Map who needs to say yes, diagnose what each of them needs to hear, and build the engagement plan that wins the room before the meeting |
| Narrative Builder | Produce the story that lands the recommendation in the first sixty seconds — Pyramid Principle, SCQA, hostile-question prep |
| KPI Architect | Replace vanity metrics with the small set of leading and lagging KPIs that actually drive strategic decisions |
| War Gaming | Stress-test the strategy against competitor response, market shifts, and execution failure before reality does |
